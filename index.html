FRAGMENTS OF NEW YORK

Exploring architecture, rhythm, texture, and urban observation through photography and design.

This project documents New York City’s built environment through repetition, ornament, cast-iron details, stone surfaces, and quiet architectural rhythm. The series connects photography with design thinking by observing how structure, pattern, and atmosphere shape the way we experience the city.

Process

My process begins with observation: walking, framing, cropping, and identifying repeated visual systems in the city.

Observation

I look for rhythm in windows, columns, facades, shadows, and ornament, treating the city as a visual system.

Composition

I use cropping, symmetry, repetition, and negative space to turn architectural details into structured visual studies.

Design Connection

My photography connects to UI/UX and graphic design through hierarchy, pattern, layout, and visual storytelling.

About

I’m Prachi Patel, a New York-based designer and photographer exploring the relationship between architecture, visual systems, and digital experience. My background in computer science, graphic design, and photography informs my interest in interaction, structure, rhythm, and urban observation. Currently completing a B.S. in Computer Science with a minor in Graphic Design at Pace University in New York City.
